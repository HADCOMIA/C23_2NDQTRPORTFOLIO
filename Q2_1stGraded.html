PAHGKAIN RECOMMENDATIONS

Ajisaki | Japanese Cusine

Pros & Cons

    Pros: relatively cheap, good food
    Cons: long waiting time due to popularity (~20 mins sometimes)

Ajisaki is a classic for many pisay students: it's fast, delicious, and relatively cheap.

Personal recommendations:

  1. Tonkatsu (100 takeout, 90 dine in) - fried pork w/ japanese-style sauce (can add on colesaw and mayo for free)
  2. Gyoza (25 EACH PIECE takeout and dine in) - pork and vegtable dumplings with soy sauce (can add chili oil for 15)
  3. Katsu Curry (105 takeout, 95 dine in) - fried pork w/ curry sauce carrots and potatoes (can add on colesaw and mayo for free)

Colais | Filipino Cusine

Pros & Cons

    Pros: relatively cheap, good food, healthier options, more options in general
    Cons: long waiting time due to popularity (most teachers buy here as well, so ~10-15 mins)

Colais offers far more options than Ajisaki, which is why when I've had enough of Aji I usually just swap over to Colais,

as their menu changes depending on the day, leaving with me with not just more, but healthier choices. They also have really

good shakes for cheap with a lot of decent options.

Personal recommendations:

  1. Dinuguan w/ rice (95 takeout, 85 dine in)
  2. Pork Siniggang w/ rice (95 takeout, 85 dine in)
  3. Sourcream Fried Chicken w/ rice (100 takeout, 90 dine in)

Yummy Tummy | Snacks/Rice Meals

Pros & Cons

    Pros: large selection of snacks, average prices (mostly)
    Cons: long waiting time due to popularity (~10 mins worst), lowk unhealthy

Yummy Tummy is a recent addition this year, but it has gotten very popular. Most of their menu consists of snacks,

with a few rice meals and sandwhiches. They also have really good ice cream and juices, so great for cooling down as well.

The only issue I have with it though, is that its quite unhealthy to eat those things everyday.

Redstraw Cafe | Pizza, Waffles, Milkshakes

Pros & Cons

    Pros: pretty dang solid food
    Cons: long waiting time due to popularity (long cooking time, ~20-30mins during peak hours), lowk expensive, lowk unhealthy

Like Yummy Tummy, redstraw is also new this year. However, it quickly became popular due to the pretty good food.

However, due to the preparation time in addition to its popularity, the waiting time can be really long. Its also kind of expensive,

and also kind of unhealthy (waffles, pizza).

Personal recommendations:

  1. Beef Pizza + Mushrooms ()
  2. Nutella w/ Banana Waffle ()
  3. Curly Fries (75 | only buy if you're feeling magastos...)
